# Module 3: New Jersey Agency Law
## Agency Disclosure, Relationships & Fiduciary Duties – Extended Question Bank

### NJ Agency Disclosure Requirements (Extended Questions 1–25)

**Question 1**
According to New Jersey's Consumer Information Statement on Agency regulations, at what specific point in the client-agent interaction must the CIS be presented in writing, and what constitutes the threshold for "substantive discussions" that triggers this requirement?
**Answer Choices:**
A. At the first property showing; substantive discussions begin when price is mentioned
B. Before any substantive discussions about a specific property; substantive means going beyond general market information
C. Within 72 hours of initial contact; substantive discussions include any mention of property features
D. At the time of writing an offer; substantive discussions are limited to financial qualifications
**Correct Answer:** B. Before any substantive discussions about a specific property; substantive means going beyond general market information

**Question 2**
A licensee meets with a prospective buyer for the first time. They discuss general neighborhood prices, school districts, and then the buyer asks about a specific listed property at 123 Main Street. At what exact moment must the CIS be presented, and what happens if the licensee fails to present it before showing that property?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Before discussing 123 Main Street; failure results in automatic commission forfeiture
B. After the showing but before writing an offer; failure requires written apology to client
C. At closing; failure is not a violation if the buyer eventually purchases
D. Within 24 hours of first contact; failure requires mandatory CE course
**Correct Answer:** A. Before discussing 123 Main Street; failure results in automatic commission forfeiture

**Question 3**
A broker presents the CIS in English to a client who primarily speaks Spanish and has limited English comprehension. The client signs the form without understanding it. Which of the following statements is correct regarding compliance with NJAC 11:5-1.21?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Compliance is met because the CIS was presented in writing
B. Compliance is not met; the CIS must be in a language the client understands
C. Compliance is met if the broker verbally explains it in Spanish
D. Compliance is met because the client signed the form
**Correct Answer:** B. Compliance is not met; the CIS must be in a language the client understands

**Question 4**
A licensee is helping a family member purchase a home. According to New Jersey regulations, is the licensee required to present the Consumer Information Statement on Agency, and if so, when must it be presented relative to substantive discussions?
**Answer Choices:**
A. No, CIS is not required for transactions with family members
B. Yes, CIS must be presented before any substantive discussions, same as any other client
C. Yes, but only if the family member requests it in writing
D. No, but a simplified verbal disclosure is sufficient
**Correct Answer:** B. Yes, CIS must be presented before any substantive discussions, same as any other client

**Question 5**
A buyer attends an open house and has a detailed conversation with the hosting agent about the property's condition, potential offer price, and seller motivation. Must the agent present the CIS at that time, and what is the retention requirement for any signed acknowledgement?
**Answer Choices:**
A. No, open house attendees are not entitled to CIS
B. Yes, immediately; records must be kept for 3 years
C. Yes, before substantive discussions begin; records must be kept for 6 years
D. No, but a summary must be provided within 48 hours
**Correct Answer:** C. Yes, before substantive discussions begin; records must be kept for 6 years

**Question 6**
A commercial real estate broker is negotiating a 10-year lease for an office building. Is the Consumer Information Statement on Agency required in this transaction, and if so, must it be presented to both the landlord and the tenant?
**Answer Choices:**
A. No, CIS is only required for residential sales
B. Yes, required for both parties before substantive lease discussions
C. Yes, but only for the tenant since the landlord is the client
D. No, commercial transactions are exempt from CIS requirements
**Correct Answer:** B. Yes, required for both parties before substantive lease discussions

**Question 7**
A licensee initially represents a seller, but after the property sells, the seller asks the same licensee to help them purchase a new home. What CIS requirement applies when the agency relationship changes from seller agency to buyer agency?
**Answer Choices:**
A. No new CIS is needed; the original covers both relationships
B. A new CIS must be presented and acknowledged before buyer agency begins
C. Only an addendum to the original CIS is required
D. Verbal disclosure of the change is sufficient
**Correct Answer:** B. A new CIS must be presented and acknowledged before buyer agency begins

**Question 8**
A buyer calls a licensee on the phone to ask specific questions about a property's square footage, age of roof, and property taxes. According to NJ regulations, does this telephone conversation trigger the CIS presentation requirement, and if so, how may the CIS be delivered?
**Answer Choices:**
A. No, telephone inquiries never require CIS
B. Yes, CIS must be mailed within 3 days
C. Yes, CIS may be delivered electronically with consent before substantive discussion continues
D. Yes, but only if the caller later visits the property
**Correct Answer:** C. Yes, CIS may be delivered electronically with consent before substantive discussion continues

**Question 9**
A licensee assists a buyer in purchasing a For Sale By Owner property. The seller is not represented by any broker. Is the licensee required to present the CIS to the buyer in this scenario, and what agency relationship typically exists?
**Answer Choices:**
A. No, CIS not required in FSBO transactions
B. Yes, CIS required; licensee is buyer's agent
C. Yes, but only if the buyer signs a buyer agency agreement first
D. No, because there is no cooperating broker
**Correct Answer:** B. Yes, CIS required; licensee is buyer's agent

**Question 10**
If a licensee fails to present the CIS before substantive discussions and a transaction later results, what are the potential disciplinary consequences under New Jersey law?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Automatic license revocation
B. Fine up to $5,000 per violation
C. Mandatory ethics course only
D. No discipline if the transaction closed successfully
**Correct Answer:** B. Fine up to $5,000 per violation

**Question 11**
How does the CIS differ from an exclusive agency agreement in terms of legal purpose and effect?
**Answer Choices:**
A. CIS creates the agency relationship; the agreement confirms it
B. CIS explains agency options; the agreement establishes the specific relationship
C. Both documents serve identical purposes
D. CIS is for residential; agreement is for commercial
**Correct Answer:** B. CIS explains agency options; the agreement establishes the specific relationship

**Question 12**
A licensee is involved in a real estate auction. What CIS requirements apply, and when must the disclosure be made to potential bidders?
**Answer Choices:**
A. CIS not required for auctions
B. CIS must be given to all registered bidders before bidding begins
C. CIS only required for the winning bidder at closing
D. Verbal announcement at the auction is sufficient
**Correct Answer:** B. CIS must be given to all registered bidders before bidding begins

### Dual Agency Scenarios & Calculations (Questions 13–30)

**Question 13**
A licensee represents both the seller and the buyer in the same transaction with both parties' written consent. The seller reveals in confidence they will accept $400,000, but are listing at $425,000. The buyer asks the licensee, "What do you think the seller will really take?" How must the licensee respond under NJ dual agency rules?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Disclose the $400,000 figure to help the buyer
B. Say "I cannot share confidential information, but market data suggests offers around $410,000 are competitive"
C. Advise the buyer to offer $400,000 exactly
D. Refuse to answer any pricing questions
**Correct Answer:** B. Say "I cannot share confidential information, but market data suggests offers around $410,000 are competitive"

**Question 14**
A dual agent earns a 6% total commission on a $500,000 sale. The commission is split 50/50 between listing and selling side. As the dual agent, they keep the full commission. How much do they earn, and is this permitted under NJ law?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $30,000; yes, dual agents may earn full commission with disclosure
B. $15,000; no, dual agency requires commission reduction
C. $20,000; yes, but must be split with another broker
D. $25,000; no, dual agency commission must be donated to charity
**Correct Answer:** A. $30,000; yes, dual agents may earn full commission with disclosure

**Question 15**
A buyer and seller both sign dual agency consent forms. Two days later, the buyer learns the licensee is the seller's cousin and withdraws consent. What happens next according to NJ regulations?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Transaction is void; earnest money returned
B. Licensee must choose which party to continue representing
C. Another agent from the same or different firm must take over representing the buyer
D. Licensee may continue as a neutral facilitator without fiduciary duties
**Correct Answer:** C. Another agent from the same or different firm must take over representing the buyer

**Question 16**
In a dual agency situation, which specific fiduciary duties are limited or modified compared to single agency?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of loyalty and duty of confidentiality
B. Duty of care and duty of obedience
C. Duty of disclosure and duty of accounting
D. All duties remain exactly the same
**Correct Answer:** A. Duty of loyalty and duty of confidentiality

**Question 17**
A licensee is representing both sides in a residential transaction. The inspection reveals a major foundation issue. What may the dual agent disclose to each party?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Tell the buyer but not the seller
B. Tell the seller but not the buyer
C. Disclose to both parties equally
D. Disclose only if asked directly
**Correct Answer:** C. Disclose to both parties equally

**Question 18**
A broker's office policy allows designated agency. Agent A represents the seller, Agent B represents the buyer in the same transaction. What is the broker's role, and how are confidentialities maintained?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Broker is dual agent; all information may be shared in office meetings
B. Broker is neutral supervisor; agents must not share confidential information with each other or broker
C. Broker represents the seller only; Agent B works directly with buyer
D. Broker has no role; agents act independently
**Correct Answer:** B. Broker is neutral supervisor; agents must not share confidential information with each other or broker

**Question 19**
In a designated agency scenario, Agent A (seller's agent) learns the seller will accept $50,000 less than list price. Agent B (buyer's agent) asks Agent A, "What's the seller's bottom line?" How must Agent A respond?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Share the exact amount to help the transaction
B. Say "I cannot disclose that, but the seller is motivated"
C. Tell the broker who then informs Agent B
D. Suggest the buyer offer $50,000 below list
**Correct Answer:** B. Say "I cannot disclose that, but the seller is motivated"

**Question 20**
A salesperson wishes to act as a dual agent but their broker's office policy prohibits dual agency. Can the salesperson still proceed with written client consent?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, client consent overrides office policy
B. No, must follow office policy
C. Yes, but only with broker's verbal approval
D. No, unless the broker amends the office policy
**Correct Answer:** B. No, must follow office policy

**Question 21**
Dual agency consent forms must include specific language and be signed by all parties. How long must these signed consent documents be retained by the brokerage?
**Answer Choices:**
A. 1 year
B. 3 years
C. 6 years
D. Permanently
**Correct Answer:** C. 6 years

**Question 22**
In a multiple offer situation where the listing agent also represents one of the buyers, what must the licensee disclose to all other prospective buyers?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Nothing; keep it confidential
B. That they are representing a buyer in the multiple offer situation
C. The identity of the competing buyer they represent
D. The amount of the competing offer
**Correct Answer:** B. That they are representing a buyer in the multiple offer situation

**Question 23**
A dual agent is asked by the buyer to help negotiate a lower price, and by the seller to hold firm at list price. How should the dual agent handle these conflicting requests?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Advocate for the buyer since they are making the offer
B. Advocate for the seller since they are the client first
C. Refuse to negotiate and let parties communicate directly
D. Provide market data and facilitate communication without advocating
**Correct Answer:** D. Provide market data and facilitate communication without advocating

**Question 24**
A licensee represents a buyer who wants to make an offer on a property listed by another agent in the same brokerage. What type of agency is this, and what disclosures are required before substantive discussions?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Dual agency; dual agency consent forms required
B. Designated agency; CIS and office policy disclosure required
C. Subagency; no additional disclosure needed
D. Transaction brokerage; verbal disclosure sufficient
**Correct Answer:** B. Designated agency; CIS and office policy disclosure required

**Question 25**
If a designated agency relationship breaks down because confidential information is accidentally shared between agents in the same office, what is the broker's responsibility?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Nothing; accidents happen
B. Report the breach to the Real Estate Commission
C. Reassign one party to another agent or brokerage
D. Convert to dual agency with new consent
**Correct Answer:** C. Reassign one party to another agent or brokerage

### Fiduciary Duty Calculations & Scenarios (Questions 26–35)

**Question 26**
A licensee learns a client is terminally ill and must sell quickly. The licensee buys the property at a discount without disclosing plans to resell it. The property is later sold for a $150,000 profit. Which fiduciary duty was breached, and what are the potential financial consequences?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of care; must return the profit to the client's estate
B. Duty of loyalty (self-dealing); must disgorge the $150,000 profit plus possible punitive damages
C. Duty of obedience; fine equal to the commission amount
D. Duty of confidentiality; license suspension only
**Correct Answer:** B. Duty of loyalty (self-dealing); must disgorge the $150,000 profit plus possible punitive damages

**Question 27**
A buyer's agent fails to disclose a known zoning change that will increase the property's value by 30%. The buyer purchases, then learns of the change. What fiduciary duty was violated, and what remedy might a court order?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of obedience; agent must pay the difference in value
B. Duty of disclosure; agent may be liable for damages or rescission
C. Duty of accounting; refund of commission only
D. No duty violated; zoning changes are public record
**Correct Answer:** B. Duty of disclosure; agent may be liable for damages or rescission

**Question 28**
A seller tells their agent in confidence they are going through a divorce and must sell quickly. The agent mentions this to a buyer who then offers 10% below list. Which duty was breached, and what disciplinary action might the Commission take?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of care; mandatory CE course
B. Duty of confidentiality; fine up to $5,000
C. Duty of loyalty; license suspension
D. Duty of obedience; written reprimand
**Correct Answer:** B. Duty of confidentiality; fine up to $5,000

**Question 29**
An agent is holding $20,000 in earnest money. They accidentally deposit it into their operating account instead of the trust account. The money is untouched. Which duty was breached, and what is the minimum fine for a first offense?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of accounting; $1,000 fine
B. Duty of care; $2,500 fine
C. Duty of loyalty; $5,000 fine
D. Duty of obedience; no fine if corrected within 24 hours
**Correct Answer:** A. Duty of accounting; $1,000 fine

**Question 30**
A dual agent has a duty of limited confidentiality. If the seller reveals they will not accept less than $300,000, and the buyer asks the direct question, "Will the seller take $295,000?", how may the agent respond without breaching fiduciary duty?
**Answer Choices:**
A. "No, they won't take less than $300,000"
B. "I cannot answer that, but I suggest you offer $300,000"
C. "The seller is motivated but has a minimum price in mind"
D. "Let me ask the seller and get back to you"
**Correct Answer:** C. "The seller is motivated but has a minimum price in mind"

**Question 31**
A licensee represents a buyer who purchases a property for $400,000. The licensee knew the seller would have accepted $375,000 but did not share this with the buyer. The buyer later discovers this. What fiduciary duty was breached, and what could the buyer recover?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of loyalty; the $25,000 difference
B. Duty of disclosure; the commission amount
C. Duty of care; cost of repairs only
D. No breach; pricing strategy is confidential
**Correct Answer:** A. Duty of loyalty; the $25,000 difference

**Question 32**
An agent spends 50 hours marketing a property, but the seller decides to not sell. The agent sends a bill for $2,500 for time spent. Is this permissible under NJ fiduciary duties?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, if the listing agreement included a fee for services
B. No, agents work on commission only unless otherwise agreed in writing
C. Yes, if the seller verbally agreed to pay for time
D. No, but the agent can sue for breach of contract
**Correct Answer:** B. No, agents work on commission only unless otherwise agreed in writing

**Question 33**
A buyer's agent recommends a home inspector who is the agent's brother-in-law. The inspector misses a major defect. What fiduciary duty is potentially breached, and what is the agent's liability?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of disclosure; agent is liable for the repair cost
B. Duty of loyalty (conflict of interest); agent may be liable for negligence
C. Duty of care; no liability because inspector is licensed
D. Duty of obedience; fine only
**Correct Answer:** B. Duty of loyalty (conflict of interest); agent may be liable for negligence

**Question 34**
A seller instructs their agent to not show the property to families with children. How must the agent respond under their fiduciary duties?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Follow instructions; duty of obedience
B. Refuse; duty to obey only lawful instructions
C. Follow instructions but disclose to buyers
D. Follow instructions if the seller has a valid reason
**Correct Answer:** B. Refuse; duty to obey only lawful instructions

**Question 35**
An agent fails to present an offer to a seller because they think it's too low. The seller later learns of the offer and would have accepted it. What duty was breached, and what could be the consequence?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Duty of care; license suspension
B. Duty of loyalty; civil damages for lost sale
C. Duty of obedience; fine up to $10,000
D. Duty of disclosure; mandatory retraining
**Correct Answer:** B. Duty of loyalty; civil damages for lost sale

### Agency Math & Commission Scenarios (Questions 36–40)

**Question 36**
A seller signs a listing agreement at 6% commission. The property sells for $650,000. The selling broker receives 50% of the commission, and their salesperson receives 60% of that share. How much does the selling salesperson earn?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $11,700
B. $19,500
C. $23,400
D. $39,000
**Correct Answer:** A. $11,700
*(Calculation: Total commission = $650,000 × 0.06 = $39,000. Selling broker's share = $39,000 × 0.50 = $19,500. Salesperson's share = $19,500 × 0.60 = $11,700.)*
