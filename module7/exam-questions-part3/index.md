# Complete New Jersey Real Estate Practice Exam & Explanations (2026 Edition)

## Main Exam Questions (1–20)

**Question 1**  
You are working with a customer and have found the perfect property. You are getting ready to prepare an offer when they ask, "Do we need a lawyer?" How should you answer?  

A. It is up to the customer.  
B. No, most people in this area don't use them.  
C. Yes. You should never buy a property without an attorney.  
D. I can help you with everything the lawyer would do and save you money.  

**Correct Answer**: C. Yes. You should never buy a property without an attorney.  

**Explanation**: In New Jersey, real estate transactions typically require attorney review. It is considered a best practice and often a legal requirement to have an attorney represent your interests in a real estate purchase.

---

**Question 2**  
You work for Broker A. Agent Bill, who also works for Broker A, asks you for help in closing a sale he is working on, and offers you 10 percent of his commission for the assistance. After the settlement, who will pay your commission to you?  

A. Bill, who promised you the commission  
B. Broker A  
C. The seller  
D. The co-operating broker  

**Correct Answer**: B. Broker A  

**Explanation**: All commissions flow through the broker. Agents are paid by their employing broker, not directly by other agents or clients.

---

**Question 3**  
What is the difference between a client and a customer?  

A. A client is someone you have a contract with to perform services for a fee and a customer is someone looking to buy something for no additional fee.  
B. A client is looking to buy something for no additional fee. You have a contract with a customer to perform duties for a fee.  
C. A client is someone who provides money and a customer owes money.  
D. A client has not yet begun a business relationship and a customer has already paid a fee for service.  

**Correct Answer**: A. A client is someone you have a contract with to perform services for a fee and a customer is someone looking to buy something for no additional fee.  

**Explanation**: A client has a fiduciary relationship with the agent (through a listing agreement or buyer representation). A customer receives services but without fiduciary duties.

---

**Question 4**  
Which of the following is true regarding commissions?  

A. They are due and payable to the agent within 10 days of the broker's receipt of funds.  
B. The agent who is the procuring cause will earn the commission when the sale is completed.  
C. A listing broker will earn his commission if a sale is completed within the 90-day broker protection period, even if the property is listed with another broker.  
D. All of the above.  

**Correct Answer**: D. All of the above.  

**Explanation**: All three statements are correct according to New Jersey real estate commission rules.

---

**Question 5**  
Businesses with what number of employees must be compliant with the standards of the American with Disabilities Act?  

A. Less than 100  
B. More than 14  
C. More than 49  
D. All businesses must be ADA compliant.  

**Correct Answer**: B. More than 14  

**Explanation**: The ADA generally applies to businesses with 15 or more employees for employment provisions, and to public accommodations regardless of size.

---

**Question 6**  
A licensed real estate broker can refuse to rent to the following tenants:  

A. A person from a minority ethnic group with substandard credit  
B. A single mother  
C. An older gentleman on social security  
D. A family with a section 8 voucher for the rental amount.  

**Correct Answer**: D. A family with a section 8 voucher for the rental amount.  

**Explanation**: While landlords cannot discriminate based on protected classes, they are generally not required to participate in Section 8 voucher programs unless local laws mandate it.

---

**Question 7**  
Knowing that you have been a licensed real estate agent for some time, your neighbor asks you if you could give her an idea of what her house may be worth. She is retiring and has been thinking of downsizing. You are glad to assist her, so you go into your local multiple listing service and search for comparable homes. You chose a few homes that have the most features in common with her home. Features would include size of the house and lot, number of bedrooms and baths, condition, location, age and any other information that would be helpful. You prepare a report that shows her how these homes compare to hers and how you arrived at the value based on these comparisons. What is the report that you present called?  

A. A cost approach valuation  
B. An appraisal  
C. A market analysis  
D. A supply and demand report  

**Correct Answer**: C. A market analysis  

**Explanation**: A Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) is prepared by real estate agents to estimate a property's value based on recent sales of similar properties.

---

**Question 8**  
You have just completed a settlement for a home that sold for $380,000 and you are calculating your commission. It was a 6 percent commission and a co-op broker had the listing. Your employment agreement has your commission split set at 65 percent. How much will your broker retain after he has paid you?  

A. $14,820  
B. $7,410  
C. $3,990  
D. $11,400  

**Correct Answer**: B. $7,410  

**Explanation**:  
Total commission = $380,000 × 6% = $22,800  
Your share = $22,800 × 65% = $14,820  
Broker retains = $22,800 - $14,820 = $7,410

**Question 9**  
Putting aside the rights of owners to manage their own properties, for which of the following do you need to be a licensed New Jersey real estate salesperson?  

A. Auctioning real estate  
B. Collecting rent  
C. Attempting to negotiate a mortgage loan  
D. All of the above  

**Correct Answer**: D. All of the above  

**Explanation**: All these activities require a real estate license when performed for others for compensation.

---

**Question 10**  
A franchise office running an advertisement in the local newspaper must make sure the ad includes the following statement?  

A. We are members of NAR.  
B. This office is a Full-Service agency serving the public for 23 years.  
C. Each office is independently owned and operated.  
D. Ad must state the number of offices in the franchise.  

**Correct Answer**: C. Each office is independently owned and operated.  

**Explanation**: This disclaimer is required for franchise real estate offices to clarify their legal structure.

---

**Question 11**  
If a client or customer were dissatisfied with the service they received from their salesperson, they could send a written complaint to the Real Estate Commission. Once the complaint is filed, what are the investigatory powers of the commission?  

A. Issue subpoenas.  
B. Administer oaths.  
C. Conduct hearings  
D. All of the above  

**Correct Answer**: D. All of the above  

**Explanation**: The Real Estate Commission has broad investigatory powers to address complaints against licensees.

---

**Question 12**  
Which type of ownership can only be held by a husband and wife?  

A. Tenancy in Severalty  
B. Tenancy in Common  
C. Tenancy by the Entireties  
D. Joint Tenants  

**Correct Answer**: C. Tenancy by the Entireties  

**Explanation**: This is a form of joint ownership available only to married couples in certain states including New Jersey.

---

**Question 13**  
Your municipality is building a new shopping mall. In order to safely control traffic entering and leaving the mall, they will need to remove all the homes on your block to build an access road. They will pay fair compensation for the taking. What is this process called?  

A. Eminent Domain  
B. Condemnation  
C. Seizure  
D. Escheat  

**Correct Answer**: B. Condemnation  

**Explanation**: Condemnation is the legal process by which the government exercises eminent domain to take private property for public use with compensation.

---

**Question 14**  
What is a latent defect?  

A. Something wrong with the property that the seller has disclosed.  
B. A defect that the seller has not disclosed.  
C. A defect that is hidden and cannot be seen.  
D. A problem dealing with lead-based paint.  

**Correct Answer**: C. A defect that is hidden and cannot be seen.  

**Explanation**: Latent defects are hidden problems not discoverable through ordinary inspection, as opposed to patent defects which are visible.

---

**Question 15**  
You and your family are renting a house at the Jersey Shore this summer. The landlord gave you a lease to sign explaining the terms such as move in and move out times and dates, amount of rent and other stipulations. What kind of tenancy does your rental fall under?  

A. Periodic tenancy  
B. Tenancy at will  
C. Estate for years  
D. Tenancy at sufferance  

**Correct Answer**: C. Estate for years  

**Explanation**: Also called a tenancy for years, this is a leasehold estate with a definite beginning and ending date.

---

**Question 16**  
What does it mean to alienate title?  

A. To have a lien placed against it.  
B. Sell the property and change ownership.  
C. To order an abstract of title.  
D. To disagree with the language in the title.  

**Correct Answer**: B. Sell the property and change ownership.  

**Explanation**: Alienation refers to the voluntary transfer of title or ownership from one person to another.

---

**Question 17**  
When a contract is made for an illegal purpose, such as deflating the purchase price to pay lower taxes, the contract becomes:  

A. Express  
B. Void  
C. Implied  
D. Voidable  

**Correct Answer**: B. Void  

**Explanation**: Contracts with illegal purposes or against public policy are void from the beginning and unenforceable.

---

**Question 18**  
A seller is approached by a buyer who is interested in an option contract on the seller's property. They agree to a sale price, consideration and a thirty day option term. Which of the following is true regarding an option contract?  

A. It is a unilateral contract.  
B. The buyer is the optionor.  
C. The seller can change his terms anytime during the 30 days.  
D. If the buyer cancels his option anytime during the 30 days the seller must immediately return the buyer's deposit money.  

**Correct Answer**: A. It is a unilateral contract.  

**Explanation**: An option contract gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to purchase within a specified time. Only the buyer is obligated to act if they choose to exercise the option.

---

**Question 19**  
What is the function of an acceleration clause in a mortgage document?  

A. It gives the borrower the ability to pay a loan off early.  
B. It authorizes bi-weekly payments.  
C. It allows the loan to be assumed by another qualified buyer.  
D. It gives the bank the ability to call the entire loan balance due upon default.  

**Correct Answer**: D. It gives the bank the ability to call the entire loan balance due upon default.  

**Explanation**: Acceleration clauses protect lenders by making the entire loan balance immediately due if the borrower defaults.

---

**Question 20**  
You have been pre-qualified so you can start looking at houses for sale, but the lenders tell you that due to poor credit they cannot help you. You continue to look anyway hoping for a miracle, and you find a home for sale with seller financing. The house is an investment property for sale by owner. Your down payment is sufficient but the seller considers you a bad risk due to your credit history. Finally, he agrees to hold a mortgage but at a 19 percent interest rate. What, if anything would protect you from such high interest rates?  

A. Regulation Z  
B. Anti-discrimination laws  
C. Usury laws  
D. Novation  

**Correct Answer**: C. Usury laws  

**Explanation**: Usury laws set maximum allowable interest rates on loans to protect borrowers from excessively high interest charges.

## Additional Questions from IT Braindumps (1–5)

**Question 21**  
A buyer who is purchasing a property intends to build a chain link fence in the backyard. There is a 12-year-old deed restriction that prohibits chain link fences. The licensee finds out that there are no city codes prohibiting chain link fences. Which of the following statements regarding this situation is true?

A. This city code takes priority because deed restrictions expire 10 years after a subdivision is developed.  
B. This deed restriction is more restrictive and will, therefore, take precedence.  
C. Deed restrictions always take priority over city codes.  
D. City code always takes priority over deed restrictions.  

**Correct Answer**: B. This deed restriction is more restrictive and will, therefore, take precedence.  

**Explanation**: Private deed restrictions that are more restrictive than municipal codes typically govern, as they are private agreements between parties.

---

**Question 22**  
A broker is offering a $100 gift certificate at a jewelry store to all sellers who list with the broker during a specified time period. According to the New Jersey Real Estate License Law, such a promotion is prohibited because it:

A. offers compensation for brokerage activity to unlicensed persons  
B. discriminates against sellers whose religion prohibits the wearing of jewelry  
C. makes the receipt of the free offering contingent upon sellers listing their properties with the broker  
D. discriminates against sellers who do not list with the broker during the specified time period  

**Correct Answer**: C. makes the receipt of the free offering contingent upon sellers listing their properties with the broker  

**Explanation**: Such contingent inducements can be considered illegal kickbacks or unethical inducements to secure listings.

---

**Question 23**  
In commercial property leasing, listing agents should ensure that the owner complies with:

A. the Americans with Disabilities Act  
B. market lease rates  
C. state and federal Fair Housing laws  
D. their fiduciary responsibilities to the tenant  

**Correct Answer**: A. the Americans with Disabilities Act  

**Explanation**: Commercial properties must comply with ADA accessibility requirements for public accommodations.

---

**Question 24**  
Which action would be allowed according to the Federal Fair Housing law?

A. A lender receives notice that a loan application submitted on behalf of a client has been denied. The lender knew the borrower was well qualified, but is told that area is off limits for new loans because of its crime rate.  
B. Broker A meets a new Hispanic buyer client. Broker B just sold a handful of condos to other Hispanic buyers in the Kentwood neighborhood, so broker A suggests the new buyer might be comfortable in that neighborhood.  
C. A broker informs a potential seller that there is an influx of immigrants snatching up rentals in the neighborhood, so this would be a good time to sell.  
D. A landlord receives a rental application from a prospective tenant, who came from Russia a few years ago to attend the local university. The lender denies the rental application after asking for the prospective tenant's visa and determines that it expires in 3 months.  

**Correct Answer**: D. A landlord receives a rental application from a prospective tenant, who came from Russia a few years ago to attend the local university. The lender denies the rental application after asking for the prospective tenant's visa and determines that it expires in 3 months.  

**Explanation**: Immigration status is not a protected class under Fair Housing laws, and legitimate concerns about a tenant's ability to fulfill lease terms are permissible.

**Question 25**  
Which of the following items would be prorated at closing with the credit going to the seller?

A. accrued interest on an assumed mortgage  
B. earnest money  
C. unearned rent collected in advance  
D. prepaid property taxes  

**Correct Answer**: D. prepaid property taxes  

**Explanation**: If the seller has prepaid property taxes for a period extending beyond the closing date, they receive a credit for the unused portion.

## Extra Practice Questions

**Question 26**  
Another name for an easement is:

A. Common lien  
B. Right of way  
C. Egress right  
D. Nonpossessory interest  

**Correct Answer**: B. Right of way  

**Explanation**: "Right of way" is a common type of easement granting passage through another's property and is often used synonymously with easement.

---

**Question 27**  
Which of the following best describes an easement in gross?

A. It benefits a particular piece of land.  
B. It is personal to the holder and not tied to land ownership.  
C. It can only be created by a written agreement.  
D. It automatically terminates when the property is sold.  

**Correct Answer**: B. It is personal to the holder and not tied to land ownership.  

**Explanation**: An easement in gross benefits a person or entity rather than being attached to specific land (like utility easements).

---

**Question 28**  
A tenant continues to occupy a rental property after the lease has expired without the landlord's consent. This is known as:

A. Tenancy at will  
B. Tenancy by sufferance  
C. Periodic tenancy  
D. Estate for years  

**Correct Answer**: B. Tenancy by sufferance  

**Explanation**: A tenancy by sufferance occurs when a tenant remains in possession without the landlord's consent after the lease term ends.
