## Financial Calculations & Math (20 Questions)

### Question 1: Complex Discount Points
**A building was purchased for $350,000 with a 20% down payment. The lender charges 3 discount points. How much will the buyer need to cover both items?**
- A. $70,000
- B. $74,200
- C. $76,500
- D. $78,400

**Correct Answer:** D. $78,400  
**Explanation:** Points are calculated on the loan amount, not purchase price.
- Down payment: $350,000 × 20% = $70,000
- Loan amount: $350,000 × 80% = $280,000
- Points cost: $280,000 × 3% = $8,400
- Total: $70,000 + $8,400 = $78,400

---

### Question 2: PITI Calculation with Amortization
**Ted and Carol buy a house listed at $105,000, offering $100,000. They put 20% down and get an 80% LTV mortgage at 5.5% for 30 years. Taxes: $1,494/year, Insurance: $480/year. What's their monthly PITI?**
- A. $512.40
- B. $562.75
- C. $618.90
- D. $645.30

**Correct Answer:** C. $618.90  
**Explanation:** 
- Loan: $100,000 × 80% = $80,000
- $80,000 ÷ 1,000 = 80 units
- From amortization table: 5.5% for 30 years = $5.68 per $1,000
- P&I: 80 × $5.68 = $454.40
- Taxes: $1,494 ÷ 12 = $124.50
- Insurance: $480 ÷ 12 = $40.00
- Total: $454.40 + $124.50 + $40.00 = $618.90

---

### Question 3: Tax Calculation with Mills
**A property has market value of $400,000. Assessed value is 60% of market value. Tax rate is 63⅛ mills. What is the annual tax?**
- A. $12,450.00
- B. $14,200.00
- C. $15,150.00
- D. $16,800.00

**Correct Answer:** C. $15,150.00  
**Explanation:**
- 63⅛ mills = 63.125 ÷ 1,000 = 0.063125
- Assessed value: $400,000 × 60% = $240,000
- Tax: $240,000 × 0.063125 = $15,150.00

### Question 4: Property Tax Proration
**Bill pays $980 property taxes for the year. He closes on July 15, with seller responsible for expenses on day of closing. Using 360-day year, what is prepaid tax credit to Bill?**
- A. $449.17
- B. $465.50
- C. $480.25
- D. $510.75

**Correct Answer:** A. $449.17  
**Explanation:**
- Days remaining: July 15 to Dec 31 = 5.5 months × 30 days = 165 days
- Credit: $980 × (165 ÷ 360) = $449.17

### Question 6: Capitalization Rate Application
**An apartment generates $70,000 net income annually, purchased at 5% cap rate. If net income increases to $100,000, what's the equity gain?**
- A. $400,000
- B. $500,000
- C. $600,000
- D. $700,000

**Correct Answer:** C. $600,000  
**Explanation:**
- Original value: $70,000 ÷ 0.05 = $1,400,000
- New value: $100,000 ÷ 0.05 = $2,000,000
- Gain: $2,000,000 - $1,400,000 = $600,000
