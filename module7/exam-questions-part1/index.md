### **Question 1**
**Question**:  
Cameron and Charlotte offer $420,000 to buy a house that is listed at $425,000. Their offer is accepted, and they obtain a loan with an 80% loan-to-value ratio. The loan is for 30 years with a 7.7% interest rate. Their PI payment is $2,410. Taxes for the year are $4,200; property insurance for the year is $792. Calculate their monthly PITI payment.

**Answer Choices**:  
A. $1,672  
B. $2,760  
C. $2,826  
D. $3,202  

**Correct Answer**: C. $2,826  

**Explanation**:  
Monthly taxes = $4,200 / 12 = $350  
Monthly insurance = $792 / 12 = $66  
PITI = $2,410 + $350 + $66 = $2,826

### **Question 2**
**Question**:  
A home's property taxes are $3,546 annually, paid in arrears. Settlement is held on March 31, and the taxes are prorated as of that date. How will the property taxes be prorated on the settlement statement (using a statutory year)?

**Answer Choices**:  
A. $886.50 Credit Buyer / $886.50 Debit Seller  
B. $886.50 Credit Seller / $886.50 Debit Buyer  
C. $2,659.50 Credit Buyer / $886.50 Debit Seller  
D. $2,659.50 Credit Seller / $886.50 Debit Buyer  

**Correct Answer**: C. $2,659.50 Credit Buyer / $886.50 Debit Seller  

**Explanation**:  
Daily rate = $3,546 / 360 = $9.85  
Seller days (Jan–Mar) = 90 days  
Seller share = 90 × $9.85 = $886.50 debit  
Buyer credit = $886.50  
*(Taxes paid in arrears: buyer pays seller's share)*

### **Question 4**
**Question**:  
Cher and Bob have a stable monthly income of $8,400. Every month, they pay $600 on car loans, $200 on an outstanding student loan, $45 for streaming services, and $90 for a gym membership. What is the maximum monthly mortgage payment they would qualify for on a conventional loan (assume a housing expense ratio of 28% and a total debt-to-income ratio of 36%)?

**Answer Choices**:  
A. $2,089  
B. $2,224  
C. $2,352  
D. $2,824  

**Correct Answer**: B. $2,224  

**Explanation**:  
Housing ratio max = $8,400 × 0.28 = $2,352  
Debt ratio max = $8,400 × 0.36 = $3,024  
Monthly debts = $600 + $200 = $800  
Max PITI via debt ratio = $3,024 – $800 = $2,224  
Lower of two = $2,224

### **Question 8**
**Question**:  
Property owner Jack lives in Big Town where the tax ratio is 80% of market value. The market value of his house is $250,000. The property tax for Big Town’s school district is 7 mills, and the property tax for the town is 9 mills. What will Jack pay in annual property taxes?

**Answer Choices**:  
A. $1,800  
B. $2,250  
C. $3,200  
D. $4,000  

**Correct Answer**: C. $3,200  

**Explanation**:  
Assessed value = $250,000 × 0.80 = $200,000  
Total mills = 7 + 9 = 16  
Tax rate = 16 / 1,000 = 0.016  
Tax = $200,000 × 0.016 = $3,200
