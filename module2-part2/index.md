# Module 2: New Jersey License Law
## NJ Real Estate License Act, Regulations & Ethics – Extended Question Bank

### NJ Real Estate License Act (Extended Questions 1–25)

**Question 1**
Under New Jersey’s Real Estate License Act, the statutory authority for licensing, regulating, and disciplining real estate professionals is codified in two primary sources. Which of the following correctly identifies both the statutory title in which the Act is found and the administrative code that implements its provisions?
**Answer Choices:**
A. NJ Statutes Title 11 and NJ Administrative Code Title 45
B. NJ Statutes Title 45 and NJ Administrative Code Title 11
C. NJ Statutes Title 13 and NJ Administrative Code Title 5
D. NJ Statutes Title 5 and NJ Administrative Code Title 13
**Correct Answer:** B. NJ Statutes Title 45 and NJ Administrative Code Title 11

**Question 2**
A property manager in New Jersey is responsible for collecting rent, scheduling repairs, and showing vacant units to prospective tenants. According to the License Act, which of these activities would require the individual to hold an active real estate salesperson or broker license?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Collecting rent and scheduling repairs only
B. Showing vacant units to prospective tenants only
C. All of the listed activities
D. None of the activities require a license
**Correct Answer:** B. Showing vacant units to prospective tenants only

**Question 3**
An auctioneer in New Jersey is hired to auction a collection of antique furniture and a single-family home on the same property. Under the License Act, which of the following statements is correct regarding licensing requirements?
**Answer Choices:**
A. The auctioneer needs a real estate license only if the home sells for more than $500,000
B. The auctioneer needs a real estate license for the home auction but not for the furniture
C. The auctioneer needs a real estate license for both the home and the furniture auction
D. The auctioneer never needs a real estate license if they are a licensed auctioneer
**Correct Answer:** B. The auctioneer needs a real estate license for the home auction but not for the furniture

**Question 4**
An unlicensed individual assists a friend in selling their home by showing the property to potential buyers, discussing offer amounts, and preparing a purchase agreement using a standard form. Which of these actions, if performed for compensation, would constitute unlicensed activity under NJ law?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Showing the property only
B. Discussing offer amounts only
C. Preparing the purchase agreement only
D. All of the above actions
**Correct Answer:** D. All of the above actions

**Question 5**
If an unlicensed person is found to have engaged in activities requiring a real estate license in New Jersey, they may be subject to both civil penalties and criminal charges. What is the maximum civil penalty per violation, and what level of criminal offense may they be charged with?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $5,000 civil penalty; felony offense
B. $10,000 civil penalty; misdemeanor offense
C. $15,000 civil penalty; disorderly persons offense
D. $20,000 civil penalty; petty disorderly persons offense
**Correct Answer:** B. $10,000 civil penalty; misdemeanor offense

**Question 6**
A candidate who is 20 years old has completed all pre-licensing education and passed the state examination. Can this candidate be issued a broker license in New Jersey, and if not, what is the minimum age requirement and additional experience needed?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, issued immediately
B. No, must be 21 and have 1 year of salesperson experience
C. No, must be 21 and have 3 years of salesperson experience
D. No, must be 25 and have 5 years of real estate experience
**Correct Answer:** C. No, must be 21 and have 3 years of salesperson experience

**Question 7**
A broker wishes to open a branch office in a neighboring town. According to NJAC 11:5-1.2, what are the two primary requirements that must be met before the branch office can legally operate?
**Answer Choices:**
A. File a notice with the Commission and pay a $50 fee
B. Obtain a separate branch office license and designate a supervising broker
C. Register the branch with the local municipality and obtain a business license
D. Notify the Commission within 30 days and post the broker’s license at the branch
**Correct Answer:** B. Obtain a separate branch office license and designate a supervising broker

**Question 8**
A licensee moves from one broker to another. According to New Jersey regulations, what specific written documentation is required to effectuate this transfer, and within what timeframe must the new broker notify the Commission?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Written consent from both brokers; notification within 10 days
B. Written consent from the salesperson only; notification within 5 days
C. Written consent from the departing broker only; notification within 15 days
D. No written consent required; notification within 30 days
**Correct Answer:** A. Written consent from both brokers; notification within 10 days

**Question 9**
A salesperson wishes to work for two different brokerage firms simultaneously, handling residential transactions for one and commercial for the other. Is this arrangement permitted under New Jersey license law?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, if both brokers agree in writing
B. Yes, if the salesperson holds a dual license
C. No, a salesperson may only be licensed under one broker at a time
D. No, unless approved by the Real Estate Commission
**Correct Answer:** C. No, a salesperson may only be licensed under one broker at a time

**Question 10**
A licensed broker wishes to work as a salesperson for another broker to gain experience in a new market. Is this permitted under NJ law?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, with written approval from the Commission
B. Yes, provided the broker surrenders their broker license temporarily
C. No, a broker cannot act as a salesperson for another broker
D. No, unless the supervising broker holds a broker-salesperson license
**Correct Answer:** C. No, a broker cannot act as a salesperson for another broker

**Question 11**
A salesperson operates as an independent contractor. Which of the following must be included in the written independent contractor agreement as required by NJAC 11:5-1.23?
**Answer Choices:**
A. A guaranteed minimum annual income of at least $25,000
B. A statement that the salesperson is not an employee for tax purposes
C. A clause requiring the salesperson to work set hours each week
D. A provision that the broker will provide health insurance benefits
**Correct Answer:** B. A statement that the salesperson is not an employee for tax purposes

**Question 12**
An employment agreement between a broker and salesperson must specify several key terms. Which of the following combinations must be included according to New Jersey regulations?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Commission splits, supervision arrangements, and independent contractor status
B. Non-compete clause, vacation policy, and retirement benefits
C. Set work schedule, dress code, and office assignment
D. Marketing budget, car allowance, and error and omissions insurance
**Correct Answer:** A. Commission splits, supervision arrangements, and independent contractor status

**Question 13**
A broker’s supervisory duties are extensive under New Jersey law. Which of the following best describes the broker’s obligation regarding review of contracts prepared by their salesperson?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Review within 72 hours of signing
B. Review before the salesperson presents the offer to the client
C. Review before the contract is signed by the client
D. Review after closing to ensure compliance
**Correct Answer:** C. Review before the contract is signed by the client

**Question 14**
A broker is legally responsible for the actions of their salesperson under the doctrine of respondeat superior. This responsibility extends to:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Only actions that the broker explicitly authorized
B. Only actions within the scope of the salesperson’s employment
C. All actions of the salesperson, both personal and professional
D. Only actions that result in a written complaint to the Commission
**Correct Answer:** B. Only actions within the scope of the salesperson’s employment

**Question 15**
In New Jersey, all commission payments must be processed through the broker. If a salesperson receives a commission check directly from a client, what must the salesperson do with those funds?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Deposit the check into their personal account and report it to the broker
B. Endorse the check over to the broker within 3 business days
C. Return the check to the client and instruct them to pay the broker directly
D. Cash the check and provide the broker with their share in cash
**Correct Answer:** B. Endorse the check over to the broker within 3 business days

**Question 16**
Brokers in New Jersey are required to maintain detailed records of all transactions. What is the minimum retention period for these records, and what specific types of records must be kept?
**Answer Choices:**
A. 3 years; transaction files only
B. 5 years; trust account records only
C. 6 years; transaction files, trust account records, and employment agreements
D. 10 years; all business correspondence and advertising materials
**Correct Answer:** C. 6 years; transaction files, trust account records, and employment agreements

**Question 17**
A broker must provide salespersons with copies of their employment agreement and commission statements. Within what timeframe must commission statements be provided after the broker receives the funds?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Within 5 business days
B. Within 10 business days
C. Within 15 calendar days
D. Within 30 calendar days
**Correct Answer:** B. Within 10 business days

**Question 18**
A salesperson leaves a brokerage firm while several transactions are still pending. According to New Jersey regulations, how are commissions on those pending deals typically handled?
**Answer Choices:**
A. All commissions go to the broker
B. All commissions go to the salesperson
C. Commissions are split according to the written employment agreement
D. Commissions are held in escrow until all parties agree
**Correct Answer:** C. Commissions are split according to the written employment agreement

**Question 19**
After leaving a brokerage, a salesperson sends marketing materials to clients they worked with at the previous firm. Is this permissible under New Jersey’s rules regarding client solicitation?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, after 30 days have passed
B. Yes, if the clients are not under an exclusive agreement
C. No, never permitted
D. Yes, if the salesperson compensates the previous broker
**Correct Answer:** C. No, never permitted

**Question 20**
A real estate team wishes to advertise using a team name like “The Dream Home Team.” According to NJAC 11:5-6.1, what must be included in all team advertising?
**Answer Choices:**
A. The team name only
B. The broker’s name
C. The individual names of all team members
D. The team’s office address
**Correct Answer:** B. The broker’s name

**Question 21**
A licensee wants to pay a referral fee to an attorney who referred a client. Is this permitted under New Jersey license law?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Yes, if the attorney is licensed in New Jersey
B. Yes, if the attorney is not acting in a legal capacity in the transaction
C. No, referral fees to unlicensed persons are never permitted
D. No, unless the attorney is also a licensed real estate broker
**Correct Answer:** C. No, referral fees to unlicensed persons are never permitted

**Question 22**
Two brokers agree to cooperate on a transaction. What form of agreement is required by New Jersey regulations to formalize their cooperation?
**Answer Choices:**
A. A verbal agreement confirmed by email
B. A written agreement before services are rendered
C. A handshake agreement at the time of closing
D. No formal agreement is required
**Correct Answer:** B. A written agreement before services are rendered

**Question 23**
A broker advertises a property as having “brand new HVAC” when the system is 5 years old. This advertisement is considered:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Permissible puffery
B. Misleading advertising
C. Acceptable if the system works well
D. Legal if the seller approved the ad
**Correct Answer:** B. Misleading advertising

**Question 24**
A licensee advertises a property online without including the broker’s name. Which of the following best describes the violation?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Blind advertising, which is prohibited
B. Acceptable if the licensee’s name is included
C. Permitted for social media ads only
D. Legal if the ad is not for a specific property
**Correct Answer:** A. Blind advertising, which is prohibited

**Question 25**
A broker places a “For Sale” sign on a property that is actually under contract but not yet closed. What must the broker do to comply with advertising regulations?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Remove the sign immediately
B. Change the sign to “Under Contract”
C. Leave the sign as is until closing
D. Add a disclaimer in small print
**Correct Answer:** B. Change the sign to “Under Contract”

### Trust Fund Math & Escrow Scenarios (Questions 26–35)

**Question 26**
A broker receives an earnest money deposit of $15,000 by personal check on Monday. According to NJAC 11:5-1.28, by when must the broker deposit these funds into the trust account?
**Answer Choices:**
A. By Wednesday of the same week
B. Within 3 business days, so by Thursday
C. Within 5 calendar days, so by Saturday
D. Before the end of the month
**Correct Answer:** B. Within 3 business days, so by Thursday

**Question 27**
A broker’s trust account has a balance of $45,000 representing client funds. The broker is permitted to keep a small amount of personal funds in the account to cover bank fees. What is the maximum amount of personal funds allowed?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $50
B. $100
C. $250
D. $500
**Correct Answer:** B. $100

**Question 28**
A broker collects an advance fee of $2,500 from a client for marketing services. According to New Jersey regulations, within how many days must the broker provide the client with a written accounting of how those funds were used?
**Answer Choices:**
A. 30 days
B. 60 days
C. 90 days
D. 120 days
**Correct Answer:** C. 90 days

**Question 29**
A property management client has a security deposit of $1,800 and first month’s rent of $2,200 due on the same day. The broker deposits both into the property management trust account. What is the total amount that must be held in trust for that client?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $1,800 only
B. $2,200 only
C. $4,000
D. $0 – rent is not trust money
**Correct Answer:** C. $4,000

**Question 30**
A broker’s trust account earns $125 in interest over a quarter. According to New Jersey’s IOLTA rules, what happens to this interest?
**Answer Choices:**
A. It is paid to the broker as compensation for account management
B. It is remitted to the New Jersey Real Estate Commission
C. It is paid to the New Jersey IOLTA program to fund legal services
D. It is distributed proportionally to the clients whose funds are in the account
**Correct Answer:** C. It is paid to the New Jersey IOLTA program to fund legal services

**Question 31**
A broker fails to reconcile the trust account for four consecutive months but no funds are missing. What is the minimum disciplinary action the Commission may impose?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Written reprimand
B. $1,000 fine
C. $2,500 fine
D. 30-day license suspension
**Correct Answer:** C. $2,500 fine

**Question 32**
A broker discovers a $500 shortage in the trust account due to a bank error. According to NJ regulations, within how many days must the broker report this shortage to the Commission?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Within 24 hours
B. Within 3 business days
C. Within 10 calendar days
D. Within 30 days
**Correct Answer:** B. Within 3 business days

**Question 33**
A salesperson accepts a $5,000 earnest money check from a buyer and holds it in their desk drawer for a week before giving it to the broker. What violation has occurred?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Commingling
B. Conversion
C. Unauthorized practice of law
D. No violation if the check was secure
**Correct Answer:** B. Conversion

**Question 34**
A broker holds $10,000 in earnest money for a transaction that falls through. The buyer and seller cannot agree on who should get the funds. What should the broker do?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Return the money to the buyer
B. Give the money to the seller
C. Hold the funds until there is a written agreement or court order
D. Split the funds evenly between both parties
**Correct Answer:** C. Hold the funds until there is a written agreement or court order

**Question 35**
A broker wants to offer a “non-refundable deposit” option to buyers. Under what conditions is this permissible in New Jersey?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Never permissible
B. Permissible if the amount is under $1,000
C. Permissible with clear, conspicuous written disclosure
D. Permissible only in commercial transactions
**Correct Answer:** C. Permissible with clear, conspicuous written disclosure

### Disciplinary Math & Fine Calculations (Questions 36–40)

**Question 36**
A licensee is found guilty of misrepresentation for the first time. According to NJAC 11:5-5.1, what is the maximum fine that may be imposed for this violation?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $2,500
B. $5,000
C. $7,500
D. $10,000
**Correct Answer:** B. $5,000

**Question 37**
If the same licensee commits a second violation within three years, what is the maximum fine for the second offense?
**Answer Choices:**
A. $5,000
B. $7,500
C. $10,000
D. $15,000
**Correct Answer:** C. $10,000

**Question 38**
A broker is fined $5,000 for a first offense trust account violation. If they fail to pay the fine within 60 days, what additional penalty may the Commission impose?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Double the fine to $10,000
B. Suspension of license until paid
C. Permanent revocation
D. Criminal charges
**Correct Answer:** B. Suspension of license until paid

**Question 39**
A salesperson is disciplined for advertising violations on three separate listings. The Commission may impose a fine of up to ______ per violation.
**Answer Choices:**
A. $1,000
B. $2,500
C. $5,000
D. $10,000
**Correct Answer:** C. $5,000

**Question 40**
A broker’s license is revoked due to conversion of trust funds. How long must they wait before they can apply for reinstatement?
**Answer Choices:**
A. 1 year
B. 3 years
C. 5 years
D. Never
**Correct Answer:** C. 5 years
